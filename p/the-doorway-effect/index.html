The Doorway Effect

December 6, 2024

​

It's wild how the smallest things reveal the biggest truths.

Ever notice how you walk into a room and completely forget why you went there in the first place? Scientists call this the Doorway Effect. Something about crossing that threshold makes your brain dump its working memory.

But this isn't about your kitchen or living room.

This is about something much bigger.

I've watched countless people hit their first major success – cross that threshold they've been grinding for – only to completely forget what they originally set out to do. The mission, the vision, the purpose that drove them... gone. Just like walking through that doorway.

Let me break this down.

When you're grinding, when you're hungry, when you're still on the other side of that door – everything is clear. Your mission is sharp. Your purpose is defined. Your values are solid.

Then you cross the threshold.

You hit your first million. You get the dream job. You build the successful business. You achieve the goal.

And something shifts.

The same way your brain dumps its working memory when you walk through a door, success has this way of dumping your original operating system. Your original mission. Your original purpose.

I see it everywhere: The founder who wanted to change the world, now just optimizing for exits The artist who wanted to create truth, now just chasing likes The builder who wanted to solve problems, now just building for funding rounds

They crossed the threshold and forgot why they entered the room.

This is why most successful people become shells of who they were supposed to be. They're standing in the room of success, trying to remember what they came here for in the first place.

But here's the real edge:

Understanding this effect is the first step to beating it.

The truly great ones – the ones who maintain their edge through all the success – they do something different. They write it down. They burn it into their core. They make their mission bigger than any doorway.

Before you hit that threshold, before you cross that doorway: Lock in your mission Define your non-negotiables Anchor your purpose

Because once you cross that threshold, everything gets blurry.

The money starts flowing. The opportunities multiply. The distractions increase.

And your original clarity becomes your most valuable asset.

This isn't just about maintaining values. This is about maintaining power. Real power. The kind that comes from clarity of purpose.

The greatest tragedy isn't failing to cross the threshold. It's crossing it and becoming someone else.

Every doorway in life – whether it's success, money, power, influence – comes with this effect. The bigger the doorway, the stronger the effect.

So write it down. Burn it in. Make it unmissable.

Because the doorway is coming. And on the other side, you'll need to remember why you walked through it.

~ CR
