Unconscious

August 29, 2024

​

Your unconscious thoughts have a profound influence on how you experience life. For a long time, I didn’t grasp this fully, and it caused a lot of confusion and frustration in my daily existence. But once I started to understand how these hidden beliefs were shaping my reality, everything began to change. This realization didn’t just make sense in my head—it also led to real, tangible improvements in my life.

​

So, what exactly did I come to understand?

​

It’s not enough to simply tell yourself positive things, like “I’m going to be rich.” While these conscious affirmations can be helpful, they often don’t address the deeper, more powerful beliefs that lie beneath the surface. These unconscious beliefs might be telling you something very different, like “Being rich will bring too many problems” or “I’m not worthy of success.” These hidden thoughts are what truly shape your reality, because they’re the ones that guide your behavior and decisions without you even being aware of them.

​

To understand this better, think about the unconscious mind as the part of you that stores all the beliefs and experiences you’ve had, even the ones you’ve forgotten or repressed. These beliefs can be about anything—money, relationships, success, or even your own self-worth. Over time, these unconscious beliefs form a kind of blueprint for how you see yourself and how you respond to the world. This blueprint then determines how you react to opportunities, challenges, and the everyday events of life.

​

The tricky part is that because these beliefs are unconscious, you’re not usually aware of them. They operate in the background, influencing your thoughts, feelings, and actions in ways you don’t realize. But just because you’re not aware of them doesn’t mean they aren’t affecting you. In fact, these hidden beliefs are often more powerful than your conscious thoughts, because they’ve been reinforced over years, sometimes even decades.
​

This brings us to a crucial question: How do you bring these unconscious beliefs into the light so you can change them?

​
Many people suggest taking a direct approach, like trying to change your thoughts through willpower or positive affirmations. But because the unconscious mind is, by its nature, hidden from your conscious awareness, this approach often doesn’t work. Instead, it’s more effective to take an indirect approach, which I call the “results-to-thoughts” model.
​

Here’s how this model works, and it’s especially useful if you’re not satisfied with the results you’re getting in life:

​

Start by taking a close look at the results you’re experiencing in different areas of your life—whether it’s your health, finances, relationships, or personal fulfillment. Write down everything, including any recurring patterns or problems that keep coming up. These external results are a reflection of what’s happening in your internal world, particularly in your unconscious mind.

​

Once you have a list of these results, the next step is to trace them back to their source. Ask yourself: What belief, thought, or story could be causing this result? Set a timer for ten minutes and write down whatever comes to mind without filtering or judging it. The goal is to let your thoughts flow freely so you can tap into those deeper, unconscious beliefs.
​

By the end of this exercise, you might discover beliefs and thoughts you didn’t even know you had. These are the unconscious beliefs that have been shaping your reality, and now that they’re in the open, you can start to see how they’ve been influencing your life.

​

Once you’ve identified these unconscious beliefs, something powerful happens. By bringing them into your conscious awareness, you begin to weaken their hold on you. These beliefs no longer have the same power to control your thoughts and actions because they’re no longer hidden. This process allows you to start freeing yourself from the unconscious forces that have been dictating your reality.
​

As you do this, you’ll find that your conscious thoughts begin to have more power. You’ll start to see real changes in your life, often without having to make drastic changes to your behavior. This is because by changing the underlying beliefs that drive your actions, you naturally start making different choices and responding to situations in new ways.
​

When you bring your unconscious beliefs into the light, you begin to change the inputs (your thoughts and beliefs) that create your reality. By doing this, you open up space for new possibilities and experiences. You’re no longer stuck in a cycle of repeating the same year over and over, mistaking routine for progress. Instead, you begin to live a life that’s more open to growth, change, and new opportunities.
​

But it’s important to remember that this isn’t something you can simply read about and understand intellectually. It’s a process that requires you to actively engage with your thoughts and beliefs, to explore them deeply, and to experiment with new ways of thinking and being. This journey is about more than just understanding—it’s about experiencing the changes that come from bringing the unconscious into consciousness.

​

So, I encourage you to give this process a try. Start by examining the results in your life, trace them back to the thoughts and beliefs that might be causing them, and then bring those beliefs into the light. The only thing you stand to lose are the unconscious patterns that have been holding you back, and what you’ll gain is a life that’s more aligned with your true desires and potential.
