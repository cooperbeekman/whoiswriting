the most valuable currencies in the trust-less society

November 24, 2023

Thinking a lot about trust-less society lately.
In this world, trust is obsolete in systems.

Systems of money - Systems of people.

Trustless is a good thing

Trustless doesn’t mean no trust; it means you don’t need to rely on trust.
​
With that in mind, the question is what becomes the most valuable.
​
BTC and authentic relationships
​
Let me explain btc for those of you that dont have a full scope on it, im always surprised about how little people understand the beautiful structure of btc and why its extremely important and how similar BTC and deep, authentic relationships are.
​
Byzantine Generals' Problem is this classic issue in computer science. It's about achieving consensus in a distributed system where some members might be unreliable or even malicious. Imagine generals of the Byzantine army trying to coordinate an attack but can't fully trust the messages they receive.
​
The challenge is to find a way to make sure everyone agrees on a plan despite potential deceit or failures in communication.
​
Bitcoin’s blockchain technology essentially solves this. It uses a consensus mechanism called Proof of Work. In Bitcoin’s case, miners work to solve complex mathematical problems. When one solves it, they broadcast it to the network. Other miners verify the solution – like confirming a battle plan is legit.
​
If most agree, that block of transactions gets added to the chain. It's all about making sure everyone's on the same page, without needing to trust each individual.
​
This is key to Bitcoin's security. It's what makes it trustless – you don’t need to trust individuals, just the system itself. It’s a brilliant solution to a classic problem.
​
Alright, let's connect the Byzantine Generals' Problem to genuine relationships:

Think of genuine relationships like solving the Byzantine Generals' Problem in real life. It’s all about building trust in a situation where you can't automatically assume everyone's reliable or upfront.

In relationships, like in the Byzantine scenario, communication and actions are your proof of work. You build trust over time through consistent, honest interactions. It’s like each action is a block added to the chain of your relationship. The more blocks of genuine interactions, the stronger the chain.

Just like Bitcoin relies on the integrity of its blockchain, real relationships rely on the integrity of actions and words. Both scenarios require a system – whether it’s a blockchain or a relationship dynamic – where each part works towards a consensus of trust.

That’s the connection. Building trust in relationships mirrors solving that Byzantine dilemma.
​
So, as we step into this trustless era, it's clear – the real currencies of value aren't what we've always thought. It's Bitcoin and genuine relationships.

Bitcoin, in its essence, is a leap into a future where trust is embedded in technology, not institutions. It's a new kind of independence, a digital revolution.

And then, there's the timeless value of genuine relationships. In a world where digital trust is the norm, the authenticity of human connections becomes our anchor, our touchstone of reality.

Both are redefining what we value and trust.
​
In this trustless society, they emerge not just as currencies, but as foundations of a new era.
