You'll never find what you're not looking for

June 13, 2024

​

There are these two young fish swimming along and they happen to meet an older fish swimming the other way, who nods at them and says “Morning, boys. How’s the water?” And the two young fish swim on for a bit, and then eventually one of them looks over at the other and goes “What the hell is water?” » David Foster Wallace
​
​
​Picture This:

Two people are laying in the grass looking up at a sky full of stars. Person A knows all the constellations and Person B knows none.

Person A can point out constellations, identify planets, and even tell stories about the mythology behind each pattern. Person B sees the same stars but they are just random points of light. The difference between them is knowledge and understanding.

How does this relate to finding what you’re looking for?

​
​1. Seeing Beyond the Obvious:

• Person A: Sees the sky with clarity and purpose. Every star has a name and a place. The patterns are familiar, and there’s a story in every constellation.

• Person B: Sees only chaos. Without knowledge, the sky is just a scattered array of stars with no meaning or connection.

​
Recognizing patterns and understanding them transforms random data into meaningful information. When you know what to look for, you see beyond the surface.

​
​2. Gathering Information:

• Person A: Has spent time learning about the stars. They have read books, used telescopes, and perhaps had a mentor.

• Person B: Might have glanced at a few stars now and then but never really focused on understanding them.

​
Just like Person A, you need to gather the right information. This is the first step to making sense of any complex situation.

​
​3. Understanding the Information:

• Person A: Doesn’t just know the names of constellations; they understand how they move through the sky, how they were named, and their significance in different cultures.

• Person B: Might remember a few star names if told, but won’t grasp their connections or meanings.

​
Understanding is deeper than knowledge. It’s about seeing how information fits together and what it means in a larger context.

​

4. Applying the Information:

• Person A: Can use their knowledge to navigate, to teach others about the stars, or to enjoy a deeper connection with the night sky.

• Person B: Can only look in wonder, without the ability to apply any of the information in a meaningful way.

​
Application is the ultimate goal. It’s where knowledge and understanding meet action. Whether it’s using the stars to navigate or using market data to make a business decision, applying what you know is crucial.

​
​The Path to Success and Growth

Awareness and Curiosity:

The first step towards success is becoming aware of your surroundings, just like understanding what “water” is for the fish. Curiosity drives us to ask questions and seek out information. Person A didn’t just see stars; they became curious about them and learned their stories.

​
​Critical Thinking and Understanding:

Don’t just accept things at face value. Like Person A, take the time to understand the deeper meaning behind the information you gather. This involves critical thinking and connecting the dots, transforming random data into a cohesive understanding.

​

Application and Action:

True growth comes from applying what you’ve learned. Person A uses their knowledge of the stars to navigate and teach, turning information into actionable wisdom. Similarly, applying critical thinking to your life allows you to make informed decisions and take purposeful actions.

Most people never take a moment to critically think about their surroundings or question the everyday things in their lives. They are like the young fish, oblivious to the water they swim in. To truly grow and succeed, we must become like Person A, who not only sees the stars but understands them and uses that knowledge to enrich their life.

Success and growth come from a continuous process of awareness, understanding, and application. By questioning the world around us and seeking deeper meaning, we transform our perceptions and create opportunities for profound personal and professional growth.

So, take a moment to look around, question everything, and strive to see beyond the surface. In doing so, you’ll find the clarity and purpose needed to navigate your own journey through life.
