Why there's no leverage in Safety

November 7, 2023

Wrote this as a mid day rant and forget to hit send.
​

Slotted this into my "Download Data" power hour because, let's be real, spilling thoughts here sharpens my edge—probably does more for me than you.

Lol Just kidding...

If there's no danger, there's almost certainly no leverage. Opportunity lies where most people refuse to go.

There comes a point where the biggest risk, both quantitatively and qualitatively, lies in fact that you are playing it too safe - Or even worse

You don't even know that you're playing it safe.

All the leverage in life, where your output becomes unfathomably larger than your input, rests in the abyss of high risk, danger, uncertainty, and point blank chaos.
​
I am a poker maxi, so let me explain it like this
​
Life's like a game of poker.
​
Most are playing with the cards clutched close, betting pennies, avoiding the thrill of the bluff.
​
They play not to lose, sticking to the low-stakes side of the table.
​
The there's the other kind, the rare breed, playing the long game.

They're not just in it; they're all over it, pushing chips forward with a smirk.

When the table's tense, they're calm and collected, betting big against the tight grips of safety.

Because all the thrill and enjoyment is in the danger, they understand that this is where the LEVERAGE lies - So why would you not enjoy and embrace this pocket of risk?
​
It's about sliding that stack of chips into the middle when everyone else is holding back, eyes wide, hearts pounding.

That's where the magic is, in the space between 'safe' and 'all-in.'
​
The zone where you find leverage.
​
Because when you play it too safe, you’re just another player waiting to fold.

Playing it safe - That's just slow-motion checkmate - For your entire LIFE.
​
But when you push your chips in, take that breathless risk, you might just rake in the pot that changes the game.
​
It's a gamble, but fortune, my friend, favors the bold.

And the more you understand the variables of the game - the less LUCK is involved.
​
And that's where you find the leverage to turn the tables.
​
