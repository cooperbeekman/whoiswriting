June 18, 2024

Running Smarter, Not Harder

In Through the Looking Glass, Alice gets a crucial life lesson from the Red Queen that many of us overlook. Alice finds herself running faster and faster but staying in the same place.

Alice never quite understood how it all started. She just remembers running hand in hand with the Queen, who kept urging her to go ‘Faster! Faster!’ Alice could barely keep up and had no breath left to speak.

What puzzled Alice the most was that, despite their speed, nothing around them seemed to change. The trees and everything else stayed put. ‘I wonder if everything is moving along with us?’ thought Alice. The Queen, almost reading her mind, shouted, ‘Faster! Don’t try to talk!’

Finally, the Queen stopped and propped Alice against a tree, telling her to rest.

Alice, looking around in surprise, said, ‘Why, I do believe we’ve been under this tree the whole time! Everything’s just as it was!’

‘Of course it is,’ said the Queen. ‘What would you have it?’

‘Well, in our country,’ Alice said, still panting, ‘you’d generally get to somewhere else — if you ran very fast for a long time, as we’ve been doing.’

‘A slow sort of country!’ said the Queen. ‘Now, here, you see, it takes all the running you can do, to keep in the same place. If you want to get somewhere else, you must run at least twice as fast as that!’

Alice is demonstrating the purest form of what we like to call The Red Queen Effect

​
​Smarter, Not Harder

The Red Queen Effect: staying in the same place means falling behind. To survive, we must evolve with the systems we interact with

Darwin observed that it’s not the strongest or smartest species that survive, but those most responsive to change. In nature, not all animals evolve at the same rate. Some adapt quicker and gain a relative advantage over their competitors, increasing their odds of survival. These small gains may seem insignificant in the short run, but over generations, they compound into substantial benefits.

Everyone from entrepreneurs and Fortune 500 CEOs to best-selling authors and middle managers faces their own Red Queen. Instead of running harder, why not run smarter?

​

In business, maintaining your position often requires increasing investment. Warren Buffett described the Red Queen Effect in the textile industry. Berkshire Hathaway faced the choice of making significant capital expenditures to reduce costs. Each proposal seemed promising on paper.

But as competitors made similar investments, reduced costs became the new standard. Individually rational decisions led to collective stagnation. Everyone invested more, yet returns remained poor. It’s like a parade where everyone stands on tiptoes to see better, but no one gains a better view.

In saturated markets, the same logic applies. Companies constantly strive to outdo each other, often at the expense of long-term strategy and innovation. Instead of running harder, the key is to run smarter — finding ways to adapt and evolve that offer sustainable advantages.

We all face a Red Queen in our lives - Whether it be business, relationships, fitness, etc.

And most of the time, we know that we are exerting force in the wrong vector, and there is almost no progress being made in correlation to the effort that is being put in.

And the scariest part of this is that most people will never realize the Red Queen Effect taking place.

They'll burnout

They'll quit

They'll move onto the next thing

Never realizing that it was not their effort that had them handicapped, but the vector in which they were applying that effort

The first step in overcoming the Red Queen Effect is recognizing it. This is where most people stumble. We’re taught to value hard work and perseverance, but rarely are we taught to evaluate the direction of our efforts. It’s not just about working hard; it’s about working smart. It’s about constantly assessing whether the path we’re on is the right one.

Once you recognize the Red Queen Effect in your life, it’s time to re-evaluate your vector. Are you putting your effort into the right areas? Are you pursuing strategies that yield real progress? It might mean pivoting your business approach, changing your workout routine, or rethinking your approach to relationships. It’s about finding that sweet spot where your efforts align with meaningful results.

To truly escape the Red Queen Effect, you must be willing to adapt and evolve. This is the heart of the lesson from Alice’s journey and Darwin’s observations. It’s not the strongest who survive, but those who are most responsive to change. Look at the successful people in any field—they’re the ones who saw the need to change course and did so without hesitation. They didn’t just run harder; they ran smarter.

But if I could give you one word that was the key to understanding, recognizing, and defeating this effect in your life.
​
​Reflection

You should reflect on your life in multiple time frames.

Weeks

Months

Years

And ask yourself :
​
​Where have I been running hard but not getting anywhere?

Reflection isn’t just about looking back; it’s about gaining insight to move forward more effectively.

It’s about identifying where you’ve been running in place and where you need to change direction.

It’s about breaking the cycle of exerting force in the wrong vector and finding the path that leads to meaningful progress.
